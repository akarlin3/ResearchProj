# Theorem 2, part (i) — Label-free undetectability of the hidden channel

> # ⚠️ REQUIRES HUMAN PROOF-REVIEW — NOT MACHINE-VERIFIED ⚠️
>
> This is a **drafted** data-processing / sufficiency argument, not algebra a symbolic engine
> checked. Both the theorem (§3–4) **and** the partial-leak proposition that generalizes it (§5)
> are human-reviewable claims. The numeric companion (`detectability.py`) only verifies the
> *structural premise* — that the hidden shift leaves the observable law `P_O` (and hence the
> monitor `M`) bit-identical (`M(δ_hid=0) = M(δ_hid=0.20) = 0.007248` exactly). The **conclusions**
> below (best detection AUC = ½; the observable/hidden split of a general shift) are consistent with
> the v3 simulation (`RESULTS_C.md`: hidden-channel detection AUC = `0.500`), but consistency is not
> proof.

---

## 1. Setup and the pinned definitions (in the locked model of `THEORY_MODEL.md`)

A deployment batch is generated by `realise_deploy` (`generative.py:172`) from base draws with reported
centres `c_i` and standardised skew errors `u_i`:

```
observable channel (δ_obs):   μ_i = c_i − β·s·δ_obs        (reported point)
hidden channel     (δ_hid):   θ_i = c_i + β·s·δ_hid + s·u_i   (truth)
```

**Definition 1 (the observable σ-algebra).** Write the deployment batch as the pair `(μ, θ)` of the
observable reported points `μ = {μ_i}` and the truths `θ = {θ_i}`. The **observable σ-algebra** is

```
O := σ({μ_i}_{i=1..n}  ,  fixed known constants),
```

the σ-algebra generated by the reported points together with quantities fixed and known before
deployment (the config `cfg`, the utility, the frozen calibration reference). Because those constants
are deterministic, they contribute no events: `O = σ(μ)` up to null sets. The truths `θ` are **not**
`O`-measurable.

**Definition 2 (label-free monitor).** A **label-free monitor** is any `O`-measurable map `M = f(μ)`
(measurable `f`); equivalently, any statistic that is a function of the observable batch alone. By
the no-label discipline asserted in `monitor.py:16`, a monitor may **not** read the truths `θ` (a
label). A **detector** is any decision rule that, from `M` (equivalently from `O`), declares the batch
*stale* or *fresh*.

**Definition 3 (hidden = the `O`-invariant component — a definition, not a derived property).** Let a
deployment shift carry the calibration law `P_cal` of `(μ, θ)` to a deployment law `P_dep`. Write
`P_O` for the induced law of the observable `μ` (the push-forward onto `O`). The shift's
**observable component** is the change it makes to `P_O` (`P_O^cal → P_O^dep`); its **hidden
component** is the part that leaves `P_O` invariant (any change to the law of `θ` given `μ`, or to
anything `O`-measurable-trivial, that does not move `P_O`). We *define* a shift to be **purely
hidden** when `P_O^dep = P_O^cal`.

This is the load-bearing modelling choice, and §5 shows it is the *only* one needed: "hidden" is
**defined** as `O`-invariance, and every consequence below follows from that definition by
measure-theoretic sufficiency — not from any special feature of the Minos channels. (The Minos
`δ_hid` channel is then simply the canonical purely-hidden shift: it perturbs `θ` only, so
`P_O^dep = P_O^cal` holds *by construction* and exactly.)

## 2. The pure hidden regime

Consider the pure hidden channel: `δ_obs = 0`, and compare two regimes,

* **fresh:** `δ_hid = 0` (the calibration distribution; stale-correction regret `R ≈ 0`),
* **stale:** `δ_hid = δ > 0` (the same regret-inducing discrepancy as a matched observable shift, by
  construction — `RESULTS_C.md` shows the two channels induce comparable `R`; `R > tol`).

By Definition 3 this is a **purely hidden** shift (`P_O^dep = P_O^cal`). We claim no label-free
detector separates fresh from stale better than chance.

## 3. Claim

> **Theorem 2(i).** For any purely hidden shift (`P_O^dep = P_O^cal`; the Minos `δ_obs = 0` channel
> realises this), every label-free statistic `M = f(O)` has identical law under *fresh* and *stale*.
> Consequently every detector built from `O` has true-positive rate equal to its false-positive rate
> at every operating point; its ROC is the diagonal and its detection AUC for the event `{R > tol}`
> is exactly **½**.

## 4. Proof (data-processing / sufficiency)

**Step 1 — the observable law is invariant to the hidden channel.**
By Definition 3 a purely hidden shift leaves `P_O` fixed: `P_O^{stale} = P_O^{fresh} =: P_O`. In the
Minos realisation this is concrete — with `δ_obs = 0` the observable batch is `O = {μ_i} = {c_i}`,
which depends only on the reported centres `c_i`; the hidden parameter `δ_hid` enters **only**
`θ_i = c_i + β·s·δ_hid + s·u_i`, and `θ` is not part of `O`. (`realise_deploy` computes `μ` without
reference to `δ_hid`, so the reported points are bit-identical across `δ_hid` — verified in
`detectability.py`.)

**Step 2 — any function of `O` inherits the invariance.**
`M = f(O)` is a measurable function of `O` alone. The pushforward of a fixed measure through a fixed
map is fixed, so

```
Law(M ∣ stale) = f_# P_O^{stale} = f_# P_O^{fresh} = Law(M ∣ fresh).
```

Equivalently, the data-processing inequality gives `I(M ; δ_hid) ≤ I(O ; δ_hid) = 0` (the mutual
information vanishes because `P_O` does not depend on `δ_hid`), so `M` carries **zero** information
about which regime produced the batch.

**Step 3 — no detector has power.**
By Neyman–Pearson, the most powerful test of *fresh* vs *stale* from `O` thresholds the likelihood
ratio `dP_O^{stale}/dP_O^{fresh}`. By Step 1 this ratio is `≡ 1`. Therefore for any threshold `m*`,

```
TPR(m*) = P(M > m* ∣ stale) = P(M > m* ∣ fresh) = FPR(m*),
```

so the ROC curve is the diagonal `TPR = FPR` and the area under it is `AUC = ½`. Because Step 2 holds
for *every* `f`, no choice of label-free statistic — the default utility-divergence `M`, the
action-divergence variant, or any other function of `{μ}` — can do better than chance. ∎

## 5. Proposition (general shifts): the observable/hidden decomposition — answering "real shifts leak"

The objection to §3 is that a *real* deployment shift need not be purely hidden — a physically hidden
change may leak into observables. The following proposition shows the theorem is **not** confined to
the by-construction Minos split: it partitions *any* shift into a monitor-visible part (bounded by
part (ii)) and a monitor-invisible part (powerless by part (i)), with the partition fixed by `O`.

**Construction (factor a general shift through its observable marginal).** Let `P_cal, P_dep` be the
calibration and deployment laws of `(μ, θ)`, with observable marginals `P_O^cal, P_O^dep` and regular
conditional kernels `K_cal(·∣μ), K_dep(·∣μ)` of `θ` given `μ` (assumed to exist). Define the
**intermediate law**

```
P_mid := P_O^dep ⊗ K_cal     (deployment's observable marginal carried with calibration's θ∣μ kernel).
```

Then `cal → dep` factors into two steps:

```
P_cal  ──(A: observable)──▶  P_mid  ──(B: hidden)──▶  P_dep
       move P_O only (P_O^cal→P_O^dep),     move θ∣μ only (K_cal→K_dep),
       conditional held at K_cal            observable marginal held at P_O^dep
```

* **Step A is the observable component.** It changes only `P_O` (`P_O^cal → P_O^dep`) at fixed
  conditional. It *moves* `O`, so a label-free `M = f(μ)` can see it.
* **Step B is the hidden component (Definition 3).** It changes only the conditional `θ∣μ` at fixed
  observable marginal `P_O^mid = P_O^dep`. It leaves `O` — hence every label-free statistic's law —
  invariant.

> **Proposition (partial leak).** With the factorisation above:
>
> **(i — detectability lives entirely in A).** For every label-free `M = f(μ)`,
> `Law(M∣P_mid) = f_# P_O^dep = Law(M∣P_dep)`, so `M` cannot separate `P_mid` from `P_dep`: applying
> Theorem 2(i) to the pair `(mid, dep)`, the **hidden component B has detection AUC = ½**. All of the
> monitor's discriminating power is spent on the observable component A.
>
> **(ii — A's regret obeys the part-(ii) bound).** Because `P_mid` carries the *calibration*
> conditional `K_cal`, the per-report expected utility is the calibration one,
> `g_τ^mid(μ) = E[U(a*(μ;τ),θ)∣μ] = g_τ^cal(μ)`. Hence
> `EU(τ;P_mid) − EU(τ;P_cal) = ∫ g_τ^cal d(P_O^dep − P_O^cal)`, and the optimality chain of part (ii)
> gives the **same** envelope verbatim,
> `R_A ≤ L · δ(P_O^dep, P_O^cal)` with `(δ,L) ∈ {(W₁, L_U), (TV, 2·osc g_τ^cal)}`.

**Net statement.** A label-free monitor is **boundedly good on the observable component of any shift
(A) — exactly the part that touches `O` — and provably powerless on its hidden component (B) — exactly
the part that does not.** Detectability and the part-(ii) bound cover the same set (the `O`-moving
component); the part-(i) impossibility covers its complement (the `O`-invariant component). The Minos
channels are the two pure special cases: `δ_obs` is a shift with `B` trivial (pure A), `δ_hid` a shift
with `A` trivial (pure B). A "leaky" real shift has both: its leakage *is* component A and is the
reason it is detectable at all (governed by part (ii)); the irreducible remainder is component B, the
genuinely hidden part, on which no label-free statistic beats chance. The "real shifts leak" objection
is therefore not a counterexample but an instance — leakage is precisely what the observable bound
prices, and the impossibility quantifies what is left.

## 6. Scope, assumptions, and the honest caveat

* **What is assumed.** Theorem 2(i) assumes a *purely* hidden shift leaves `P_O` **exactly** invariant
  — which is now a **definition** (Definition 3), not a claim about physics. §5 removes the need to
  assume the *whole* shift is hidden: a general shift is decomposed, and only its `O`-invariant
  *component* is asserted undetectable. The construction in §5 assumes regular conditional kernels
  `K_cal, K_dep` exist (standard for these laws).
* **What is not claimed.** Nothing is claimed about the *size* of the hidden component for any
  particular real shift — only that whatever part is `O`-invariant is undetectable, and whatever part
  is `O`-moving falls under the part-(ii) bound. The decomposition is exact; the apportionment between
  A and B is shift-specific.
* **Why it matters.** Parts (i)+(ii), with §5, say: a label-free monitor can be made arbitrarily good
  on the observable component (`R_obs ≤ L·δ`, part ii) and is provably powerless on the hidden
  component (AUC = ½, part i) — for *any* shift, not just the engineered split. The hidden component
  induces regret yet is invisible. This is the principled argument for **periodic labeled
  repeatability spot-checks** alongside the monitor: only a statistic that reads `θ` (a label) can see
  the hidden component, precisely because every function of the observables cannot.

## 7. For the reviewer

Points to scrutinise:
1. **Definition 1/2.** Is "label-free monitor = `O`-measurable function of `O = σ({μ_i}, fixed known
   constants)`" the right formalisation of the no-label discipline? (It matches the code's assertion
   `monitor.py:16`; it is now pinned as a definition for the paper rather than left to prose.)
2. **Definition 3 (the load-bearing choice).** "Hidden = the `O`-invariant component of a shift" is a
   *definition*, not a derived property. The theorem's force depends on accepting it. §5 is designed so
   that this is the *only* modelling commitment: everything else is sufficiency. Is the definition the
   right one, and is §5's claim that nothing further is assumed correct?
3. **§5 factorisation.** The intermediate law `P_mid = P_O^dep ⊗ K_cal` and the two-step factorisation
   are the new content. Check: (a) Step B genuinely leaves `P_O` invariant (it does:
   `P_O^mid = P_O^dep` by construction); (b) the application of Theorem 2(i) to `(mid, dep)` is valid
   (they share the observable marginal `P_O^dep`); (c) part (ii)'s envelope really does apply *verbatim*
   to step A because `g_τ^mid = g_τ^cal` (the conditional is held at `K_cal`). These are the steps a
   referee should verify by hand.
4. **Population vs finite sample.** The AUC = ½ statement is for the *population* ROC. The v3 empirical
   AUC = `0.500` is a finite-sample estimate; the proof concerns the idealised law. No finite-sample
   concentration claim is made here.
