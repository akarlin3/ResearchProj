## 1. Setup and definitions (in the locked model of `THEORY_MODEL.md`)

A deployment batch is generated by `realise_deploy` (`generative.py:172`) from base draws with reported
centres `c_i` and standardised skew errors `u_i`:

```
observable channel (δ_obs):   μ_i = c_i − β·s·δ_obs        (reported point)
hidden channel     (δ_hid):   θ_i = c_i + β·s·δ_hid + s·u_i   (truth)
```

**Definition 1 (label-free monitor).** A *label-free monitor* is any measurable map `M = f(O)` of the
*observable* batch `O = {μ_i}_{i=1..n}` (it may also use the known, fixed `cfg`/utility and the frozen
calibration reference, but it may **not** read the truths `{θ_i}` — the no-label discipline asserted in
`monitor.py:16`). A *detector* is any decision rule that, from `M` (equivalently from `O`), declares the
batch *stale* or *fresh*.

**Definition 2 (the hidden channel).** The *hidden* component of a deployment shift is, by definition,
the part that leaves the observable law `P_O` **exactly invariant**. In the Minos-Core split this is
realised by `δ_hid`, which enters only `θ_i` and never `μ_i` — so the reported points are unchanged.
(This makes the exact invariance a *definition* of "hidden", not a property to be argued; the genuinely
observable-moving part of any real shift is handled separately by Theorem 2(ii) and the "partial leak"
proposition.)

Consider the pure hidden channel: `δ_obs = 0`, and compare two regimes,

* **fresh:** `δ_hid = 0` (the calibration distribution; stale-correction regret `R ≈ 0`),
* **stale:** `δ_hid = δ > 0` (the same regret-inducing discrepancy as a matched observable shift, by
  construction — `RESULTS_C.md` shows the two channels induce comparable `R`; `R > tol`).

We claim no label-free detector separates fresh from stale better than chance.

## 2. Claim

> **Theorem 2(i).** For the hidden channel (`δ_obs = 0`), every label-free statistic `M = f(O)` has
> identical law under *fresh* and *stale*. Consequently every detector built from `O` has
> true-positive rate equal to its false-positive rate at every operating point; its ROC is the
> diagonal and its detection AUC for separating fresh from stale (equivalently, for the event
> `{R > tol}`) is exactly **½**.

## 3. Proof (data-processing / sufficiency)

**Step 1 — the observable law is invariant to the hidden channel (the premise).**
With `δ_obs = 0`, the observable batch is `O = {μ_i} = {c_i}`, which depends only on the reported
centres `c_i`. The hidden parameter `δ_hid` enters **only** `θ_i = c_i + β·s·δ_hid + s·u_i`, and `θ` is
not part of `O`. Hence the joint distribution of `O` is the same under both regimes:

```
P_O^{stale} = P_O^{fresh} =: P_O.
```

This is exact, not approximate: `realise_deploy` computes `μ` without reference to `δ_hid`, so the
reported points are **bit-identical** across `δ_hid`. **Machine-verified** in
`impossibility_check.py` across 16 seeds and the full sweep `δ_hid ∈ [0, 0.24]`:
`max|μ(δ_hid) − μ(0)| = 0` exactly (so a fortiori `max|M(δ_hid) − M(0)| = 0`).

**Step 2 — any function of `O` inherits the invariance.**
`M = f(O)` is a measurable function of `O` alone. The pushforward of a fixed measure through a fixed
map is fixed, so

```
Law(M ∣ stale) = f_# P_O^{stale} = f_# P_O^{fresh} = Law(M ∣ fresh).
```

Equivalently, the data-processing inequality gives `I(M ; δ_hid) ≤ I(O ; δ_hid) = 0` (the mutual
information vanishes because `P_O` does not depend on `δ_hid`), so `M` carries **zero** information
about which regime produced the batch.

**Step 3 — no detector has power.**
By Neyman–Pearson, the most powerful test of *fresh* vs *stale* from `O` thresholds the likelihood
ratio `dP_O^{stale}/dP_O^{fresh}`. By Step 1 this ratio is `≡ 1`. Therefore for any threshold `m*`,

```
TPR(m*) = P(M > m* ∣ stale) = P(M > m* ∣ fresh) = FPR(m*),
```

so the ROC curve is the diagonal `TPR = FPR` and the area under it is `AUC = ½`. Because Step 2 holds
for *every* `f`, no choice of label-free statistic — the default utility-divergence `M`, the
action-divergence variant, or any other function of `{μ}` — can do better than chance. ∎

## 3a. Finite-sample statement (no asymptotics needed)

The population statement above is matched by an exact finite-sample one, which is what a deployment
actually computes. Let a detection set comprise `n` fresh batches and `n` stale batches over the same
seeds, scored by `M`. Because `M(fresh, seed) = M(stale, seed)` **exactly** (Step 1, pathwise), the
two groups are the *same multiset* of scores; the Mann–Whitney U statistic of two identical samples is
exactly ½ (every cross-pair is a tie, counted at ½). More generally, when fresh and stale scores are
exchangeable, the empirical AUC is an unbiased estimator of ½ for **every** `n`, with variance
`O(1/n)`. **Machine-verified**: the fresh-vs-stale AUC is `0.500000` and the bootstrap 95% CI over
seeds is the degenerate `[0.500000, 0.500000]` (`impossibility_check.py`); the v3 regret-detection AUC
on the hidden channel is `0.5000`, matching `RESULTS_C.md` (`0.500`). No finite-sample concentration
*asymptotics* are invoked — the estimator is pinned to ½ by the exact tie structure.

## 4. Scope, assumptions, and the honest caveat (preserved)

* **What is assumed.** The hidden channel leaves the observable law `P_O` **exactly** invariant. In the
  Minos-Core split this holds *by construction* (`δ_hid` perturbs `θ` only), and we adopt it as the
  *definition* of "hidden" (Definition 2). The theorem is sharp for this idealisation — it is the
  definition that gives the result its force, not an empirical claim about every real-world shift.
* **What is not claimed.** Real deployment shifts need not factor so cleanly: a physically "hidden"
  change may leak into observables (e.g. correlated acquisition features), in which case `P_O` *does*
  move and the observable-channel bound of part (ii) applies to whatever leaks (this is exactly the
  "partial leak" proposition). The theorem bounds the *genuinely* hidden component — the part that, by
  definition, leaves every observable summary untouched.
* **Why it matters.** Parts (i)+(ii) together say: a label-free monitor can be made arbitrarily good on
  the observable component (`R_obs ≤ L·δ`, part ii) and is provably powerless on the hidden component
  (AUC = ½, part i). The hidden component induces *the same regret* yet is invisible. This is the
  principled argument for **periodic labeled repeatability spot-checks** alongside the monitor: only a
  statistic that reads `θ` (a label) can see the hidden channel, precisely because every function of
  the observables cannot.
