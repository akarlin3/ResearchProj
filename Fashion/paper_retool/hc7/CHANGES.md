## Track A — demonstrate (CP1; the decisive, cheap test)

- `Sextant/scripts/run_phantom_recovery.py`: added `B4_flag_utility`. On the same
  seeded truth-controlled substrate as the existing B3 (N=3000/SNR, Fashion's exact
  NLLS + rail thresholds), it scores railing as a per-voxel flag of an **unreliable
  D\*** (truth-referenced, convention-free: normalised error $>0.5$): precision,
  recall, lift, balanced accuracy, and the **exclusion benefit** (pooled D\* error
  with vs without railed voxels), all with 2000-resample bootstrap CIs, at
  SNR 10/20/40. B1/B2/B3 reproduce **bit-identically** (only B4 added).
- Result (honesty gate, full detail in `ADVERSE_RESULTS.md`): railing is
  **specific, not sensitive** — precision 0.50–0.68 (lift 1.27–1.70), recall
  0.28–0.39, excluding railed voxels cuts pooled D\* error 10–15% (CI excludes 0).
  **Not null**; "diagnostic" is earned for *flagging* untrustworthy D\*.
