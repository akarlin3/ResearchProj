\begin{abstract}
\noindent
A deployed quantitative-MRI parameter map carries an error bar. Split-conformal
prediction makes that bar \emph{marginally} valid for free, even when the fitted
model is wrong~\citep{lei2018,vovk2005}. We show, on a fully synthetic
intravoxel-incoherent-motion (IVIM) digital reference object, that this guarantee
hides a failure that matters in practice: fitting a \emph{bi-exponential} model on
\emph{non-bi-exponential} truth leaves marginal coverage of the well-identified
tissue-diffusion coefficient $D$ intact ($\numMarg$ at nominal $\numNominal$), yet
breaks its \emph{conditional} coverage along a latent, heavy-tailed
perfusion-misspecification axis (conditional gap $\numGap$, 95\%\,CI
$[\numGapLo,\,\numGapHi]$). The failure is mechanistically distinct from a
within-model identifiability limit: under the \emph{exact} partition a companion
identifiability study uses to define its ``trust $D$'' region~\citep{gauge}\PROV{}
--- the bottom-two terciles of true $\Dstar$ --- the gap does not vanish but
\emph{intensifies} (well-identified gap $\numWell$, $[\numWellLo,\,\numWellHi]$;
strictly inside the identifiable region, $\numStrictLo$), while in that study's own
ill-posed high-$\Dstar$ regime the gap is near zero ($\numWallGap$). The driver is
a signed bias of $\hat{D}$ that grows $\sim$\,$\numBias\times$ with departure as
the heavy-tailed perfusion term aliases into the high-$b$ tissue slope. We pair the
characterization with an observable, ground-truth-free diagnostic that reads the
bi-exponential fit's residual \emph{structure}; it detects the heavy-tail channel
(AUC $\numAUC$, $[\numAUCLo,\,\numAUCHi]$), beating a naive deployment-drift monitor
($\numMonitor$), but---reported honestly---pure perfusion dispersion stays a
near-hidden channel (AUC $\numAUCdisp$, below the pre-registered $\numAUCfloor$
floor): the contribution is a \emph{heavy-tail misspecification detector}, not a
universal one. The separation is robust across SNR $\numSNRmin$--100, cohort sizes,
and $b$-schemes ($\numCondSurv/\numCondTot$ conditions), with one stated boundary:
it disappears at SNR $\numSNRfail$, where noise dominates the bias. All results are
seed-generated with no clinical data and make no clinical claim.
\end{abstract}

\section{Introduction}
% =============================================================================================
Quantitative MRI increasingly ships a parameter \emph{map} with a per-voxel
uncertainty. Conformal prediction is an attractive way to put a distribution-free
guarantee on that uncertainty: under exchangeability, split-conformal intervals
attain a user-specified \emph{marginal} coverage in finite samples for \emph{any}
underlying predictor, including a biased or misspecified one~\citep{vovk2005,lei2018}.
A practitioner could reasonably read this as a safety net: even if the IVIM
bi-exponential model is not exactly the physics, the conformal error bar still
covers at the nominal rate.

That reading is a trap. Marginal coverage is an average over the cohort; it can be
held exactly while coverage \emph{conditional} on a clinically relevant axis fails
badly~\citep{barber2021}. The question this paper answers is concrete: when the
fitted model is \emph{wrong}---bi-exponential fit, non-bi-exponential truth---which
conditional axis does the conformal bar fail along, does that failure touch the
parameter practitioners are told to \emph{trust}, and can a deployer see it coming
from observables alone?

We make three contributions, each pre-registered with a refute condition and
reported with a bootstrap confidence interval.
\begin{enumerate}\itemsep2pt
\item \textbf{The misspecification instance (\S\ref{sec:break}).} On a synthetic
  IVIM digital reference object, a heavy-tailed (stretched-exponential) perfusion
  departure leaves the \emph{marginal} coverage of the well-identified tissue
  coefficient $D$ at nominal but breaks its \emph{conditional} coverage along the
  latent departure axis. The mechanism is a signed bias of $\hat{D}$ that grows
  $\sim$\,$\numBias\times$ as the heavy perfusion tail aliases into the high-$b$
  tissue slope; a faster tri-exponential pool, which decays \emph{away} from high
  $b$, produces no such failure (a pre-registered null).
\item \textbf{Distinct from identifiability (\S\ref{sec:gauge}).} The failure is
  \emph{not} a restatement of the within-model identifiability wall that afflicts
  $\Dstar$ at high $\Dstar$~\citep{gauge}\PROV{}. Under that study's own partition
  of the latent axis, the gap on $D$ survives and \emph{intensifies} inside the
  identifiable region it calls safe, while it is near zero in the ill-posed regime
  ---an \emph{opposite} gradient. Misspecification ($\neq$\,identifiability) of a
  \emph{different}, well-identified parameter is the wedge.
\item \textbf{An observable diagnostic, honestly scoped (\S\ref{sec:diag}).} A
  label-free reading of the bi-exponential fit's residual \emph{structure} detects
  the heavy-tail channel (beating a naive deployment-drift monitor), while pure
  dispersion stays near-hidden. We lock the claim to a \emph{heavy-tail
  misspecification detector}, not a universal one.
\end{enumerate}

The throughline is that model misspecification is invisible from inside the model:
the marginal guarantee holds, the trusted parameter's point estimate looks
ordinary, and only a structured residual---or knowledge of the latent
departure---reveals that the error bar has been silently aliased.

\section{Background and related work}\label{sec:bg}
% =============================================================================================
\paragraph{Conformal marginal robustness (the baseline we confirm, not the contribution).}
Split-conformal prediction yields finite-sample marginal coverage under
exchangeability for any base predictor~\citep{vovk2005,lei2018}; the guarantee is
robust to model misspecification \emph{marginally}. Our marginal result
(\S\ref{sec:break}) is a confirmation of this classical fact and is explicitly
\emph{not} the contribution; we report it to delimit where the guarantee stops.

\paragraph{Conditional coverage is not free.} \citet{barber2021} prove that exact
distribution-free \emph{conditional} coverage is impossible without further
assumptions. This is the theoretical reason a latent axis \emph{can} carry a
coverage failure invisibly; our work is a concrete, mechanistic IVIM instance of
that impossibility under model misspecification.

\paragraph{Identifiability of $\Dstar$ vs.\ misspecification of $D$.}
A companion line of work~\citep{gauge}\PROV{} establishes that the IVIM
pseudo-diffusion coefficient $\Dstar$ is conditionally under-covered in its
\emph{high}-$\Dstar$ tercile because the Fisher information for $\Dstar$ collapses
there (a Cram\'er--Rao identifiability wall): the model is assumed \emph{correct},
and the failure is a property of estimating $\Dstar$. Our failure is orthogonal on
both axes: it is the coverage of the \emph{tissue} coefficient $D$ (the parameter
that study marks well-identified and trustworthy), it occurs \emph{because the
model is wrong}, and it lives in the \emph{low}-$\Dstar$ region that study calls
safe. \S\ref{sec:gauge} makes the distinction quantitative under that study's exact
partition. % FORWARD-CITE-gauge: Gauge is an in-review companion; see refs.bib (@unpublished) + release_config.json.

\paragraph{Misspecification diagnostics in qMRI.} Goodness-of-fit and model-order
selection (AIC/BIC/$F$-test for mono/bi/tri/stretched IVIM) ask which model to fit;
a misspecified-Cram\'er--Rao variance-consistency test has been proposed for
arterial spin labeling~\citep{wangtamirbush2026}. Our diagnostic differs in target:
it does not pick a model order, it predicts \emph{where the conditional coverage of
a trusted parameter fails}, from the deployed bi-exponential fit's residual
structure, in IVIM/diffusion rather than ASL. IVIM uncertainty quantification by
calibration curves and CRPS~\citep{casali2026} addresses a different question
(aleatoric/epistemic UQ) with no conformal coverage and no misspecification axis.

\section{Methods}\label{sec:methods}
% =============================================================================================
\paragraph{Synthetic ground truth (Lattice DRO).} All cohorts are drawn from a
clean-room, seed-generated IVIM digital reference object~\citep{lattice}\PROV{};
no measured data are used and no clinical claim is made. The canonical signal is
the Le Bihan bi-exponential~\citep{lebihan1988}
\[
  S(b)/S_0 \;=\; f\,e^{-b\Dstar} + (1-f)\,e^{-b D},
\]
with base parameters $(D,\Dstar,f)$ drawn from a documented physiological prior and
\emph{seed-locked across the departure sweep}, so the same voxel population is
observed under increasing departure. Three non-bi-exponential perfusion families
replace the single $\Dstar$ exponential while leaving the tissue term
$(1-f)e^{-bD}$---hence $D$---an exact ground-truth parameter:
\emph{stretched-exponential} ($e^{-(b\Dstar)^\beta}$, heavy tail; departure $\beta$),
\emph{log-normal dispersion} (a gamma/log-normal mixture; departure CV), and
\emph{tri-exponential} (a faster third pool; departure $g$). Each reduces
\emph{exactly} to the bi-exponential at its limit ($\beta=1$, CV$=0$, $g=0$), a
built-in placebo.

\paragraph{Estimator and intervals.} Every voxel is fit by a bounded
bi-exponential nonlinear least squares---deliberately misspecified whenever the
truth is not bi-exponential. We form split-conformal absolute-residual intervals
for $D$ at $\alpha=0.10$ (nominal $\numNominal$). A single \emph{departure-blind}
radius, calibrated on the pooled placebo-and-worst-departure cohort, defines both
the marginal coverage and the conditional gaps, so the marginal claim and the gap
claim share one calibration recipe.

\paragraph{The well-identified-$\Dstar$ subset $=$ the companion's identifiable region.}
To test distinctness rigorously, we define the ``well-identified'' subset by the
\emph{exact} construction the companion identifiability study uses for its latent
axis: terciles of true $\Dstar$ via \texttt{np.quantile([1/3,\,2/3])} followed by
\texttt{np.digitize}. The identifiable region (where that study says ``trust $D$'')
is the bottom-two terciles; the top tercile is its ill-posed wall. We report the
gap on the full test set, on the bottom-two terciles ($n\approx\numNwell$), on the
strict bottom tercile ($n\approx\numNlo$, the deepest interior of the identifiable
region), and---for contrast---on the top tercile.

\paragraph{Observable diagnostic and baseline.} From the bi-exponential fit residual
(no ground truth) we read three structure statistics---reduced-$\chi^2$, lag-1
autocorrelation, and longest same-sign run---and score a worst-vs-placebo detection
AUC. The baseline-to-beat is a naive label-free deployment-drift monitor (a
Mahalanobis score on observable signal-shape summaries plus the fit-residual norm,
the standard recipe), \emph{computed on the same cohorts} rather than asserted.

\paragraph{Statistics.} Headline gaps carry two-level (seed, voxel) cluster-bootstrap
95\% CIs over $\numSeedsHeadline$ seeds; AUCs and the bias ratio carry across-seed
95\% CIs over $\numSeeds$ seeds. Every number in this manuscript is regenerated into
\texttt{numbers.tex} by \texttt{paper/consistency.py} from the seeded gate outputs.

\section{Results}
% =============================================================================================

\subsection{Marginal coverage holds; conditional coverage of $D$ breaks}\label{sec:break}
Under the departure-blind radius, marginal coverage of $D$ stays at nominal across
all three families ($\numMarg$, CI $[\numMargLo,\,\numMargHi]$ for the heavy-tail
family; nominal $\numNominal$): the conformal guarantee is intact on average,
confirming~\citep{lei2018}. Yet for the heavy-tail (stretched) family the
\emph{conditional} coverage of $D$ degrades monotonically along the latent
departure axis, for an overall conditional gap of $\numGap$ (CI
$[\numGapLo,\,\numGapHi]$, $\numSeedsHeadline$ seeds). The mechanism is a signed
bias of $\hat{D}$ that grows $\sim$\,$\numBias\times$ from placebo to worst
departure: the heavy perfusion tail persists to high $b$ and aliases into the
tissue slope, biasing $\hat D$ in a direction the departure-blind interval cannot
anticipate. The bi-exponential-limit placebo row itself stays at nominal, so the
failure is departure-driven, not a binning artifact (refute R3 cleared).

\subsection{Distinct from a within-model identifiability wall}\label{sec:gauge}
The central claim is that this is not the companion study's $\Dstar$
identifiability wall in disguise. Table~\ref{tab:gauge} reports the gap on $D$
under that study's \emph{own} partition of the latent $\Dstar$ axis. Inside the
identifiable region (bottom-two terciles, where the companion says ``trust $D$'')
the gap does not collapse toward the marginal---the pre-registered refute R2---but
\emph{grows} to $\numWell$ (CI $[\numWellLo,\,\numWellHi]$), and grows further, to
$\numStrictLo$, in the strict bottom tercile deepest inside that region. In the
companion's \emph{own} ill-posed high-$\Dstar$ regime, the gap is near zero
($\numWallGap$). The gradient is therefore \emph{opposite} to an identifiability
wall (Fig.~\ref{fig:distinct}): identifiability fails where $\Dstar$ is large; our
coverage failure of $D$ concentrates where $\Dstar$ is small (slow perfusion decay
leaks furthest into the high-$b$ tissue slope). Misspecification of a
well-identified parameter, not unidentifiability, is the operative cause.

\subsection{The wedge is mechanism-specific, not generic misspecification}\label{sec:scope}
Not all misspecification breaks $D$-coverage. Table~\ref{tab:families} contrasts
the three families. The faster tri-exponential pool decays \emph{away} from the
high-$b$ tissue slope and produces no conditional failure ($\numGapTri$ overall,
$\numWellTri$ well-identified---a pre-registered null). Pure log-normal dispersion
leaks only mildly into high $b$ and gives a weak break ($\numGapDisp$). The pattern
tracks the high-$b$ aliasing mechanism precisely: only departures that push
perfusion signal into the tissue-slope regime bias $\hat D$ and break its
conditional coverage.

\subsection{An observable diagnostic, honestly scoped}\label{sec:diag}
Can a deployer detect the misspecification---and thus the risk to $D$'s conditional
coverage---without ground truth? Reading the bi-exponential fit's residual
\emph{structure} detects the heavy-tail channel at AUC $\numAUC$ (CI
$[\numAUCLo,\,\numAUCHi]$; the structure statistics alone, lag-1 autocorrelation
and longest-run, give $\numAUCstruct$), above the residual-magnitude channel alone
($\numAUCchi$) and well above the naive deployment-drift monitor ($\numMonitor$,
CI $[\numMonitorLo,\,\numMonitorHi]$), and it ranks voxels by their realized
$|D$-error$|$ (Spearman $\rho=\numRho$). Reported honestly, the reach is bounded:
pure dispersion stays a near-hidden channel (AUC $\numAUCdisp$, below the
pre-registered $\numAUCfloor$ detection floor, consistent with the naive monitor
also failing there), and the tri-exp pool is \emph{detectable} (AUC $\numAUCtri$)
yet harmless to $D$---detection and coverage-failure coincide only for the
heavy-tail channel. We therefore lock the claim to a \textbf{heavy-tail
misspecification detector}, not a universal one.

\subsection{Robustness envelope and an honest boundary}\label{sec:robust}
The heavy-tail separation holds across the realistic envelope: it survives in
$\numCondSurv$ of $\numCondTot$ swept conditions---SNR $\numSNRmin$--100 (the
well-identified gap \emph{grows} with SNR, to $\numWellSNRhi$ at SNR\,100), cohort
sizes $\numN$ up to $2000$ ($\numWellBig$ at $n{=}2000$), and acquisition schemes
from the dense default to a sparse clinical 10-point scheme ($\numWellClin$). The
one boundary, reported rather than buried: at SNR $\numSNRfail$ the separation
disappears, because there the Rician-floor noise dominates the departure-driven
bias (Fig.~\ref{fig:robust}). The effect is thus a property of the
moderate-to-high-SNR regime (default SNR $\numSNR$) where IVIM diffusion mapping is
actually trusted.

\section{Discussion}
% =============================================================================================
A distribution-free \emph{marginal} guarantee can coexist with a large,
clinically-shaped \emph{conditional} failure of the very parameter a deployer is
told to trust---when the fitted model is wrong. We exhibit this as a sharp,
mechanistic IVIM instance: heavy-tailed perfusion misspecification aliases into the
high-$b$ tissue slope, biases the well-identified $D$, and breaks its conditional
coverage \emph{inside} the region a within-model identifiability analysis marks
safe. The effect is the mirror image of an identifiability wall---same modality,
different parameter, opposite $\Dstar$ gradient, and a different root cause
(misspecification, not lost Fisher information).

\paragraph{What this is not.} It is not a claim that conformal prediction
``breaks'': marginal coverage is held throughout, exactly as the theory
promises~\citep{lei2018}. It is not the $\Dstar$ identifiability wall~\citep{gauge}:
\S\ref{sec:gauge} separates them under that study's own definition. It is not a
model-order selector or an ASL variance test~\citep{wangtamirbush2026}; it predicts
where a trusted parameter's conditional coverage fails. And it is not a universal
misspecification detector: the diagnostic is scoped to the heavy-tail channel.

\paragraph{Limitations.} The study is fully synthetic by design (a digital
reference object isolates the mechanism without confounds); it makes \emph{no
clinical claim}, and the diagnostic is moderate (AUC $\numAUC$) and
family-dependent. Pushing the diagnostic (structured-residual generalized
likelihood ratios, multi-segment consistency) and an in-vivo demonstration on an
open cohort are future work. The honest SNR boundary (\S\ref{sec:robust}) bounds
the regime of validity.
