\begin{abstract}
\noindent
Anomalous-diffusion MRI summarises tissue microstructure through a \emph{fractional order}:
the stretched-exponential heterogeneity exponent $\alpha$, or---in the continuous-time
random-walk (CTRW) / fractional Bloch--Torrey picture---a joint time-order $\alpha$ and
space-order $\beta$. We ask a prior question to estimation: \emph{can the fractional order be
recovered at all} from a finite-$b$, Rician-noise magnitude signal, estimated jointly with the
diffusion coefficient $D$ and the amplitude $S_0$? We derive the Fisher information and
Cram\'er--Rao lower bound (CRLB) for the fractional order under the $b$-indexed signal-decay
forward model and locate a \emph{recovery-collapse wall}. For the single-order
stretched-exponential model, under sparse clinical acquisition ($\numCPzeroNb$ $b$-values,
$b_{\max}=\numCPzeroBmax$\,s/mm$^2$) the wall sits at $\mathrm{SNR}^\ast=\numWallAnalytic$ (CRLB)
/ $\numWallEmp$ (bootstrap, 95\,\% CI $[\numWallCIlo,\numWallCIhi]$), \emph{inside} the cited
clinical-SNR band $[\numBandLo,\numBandHi]$, and it is robust across the physiological
$\alpha$ range $[\numAlphaPhysLo,\numAlphaPhysHi]$ (wall in $[\numWallAlphaLo,\numWallAlphaHi]$).
For the joint model at a single diffusion time, the time- and space-orders are
\emph{structurally degenerate}: the median $|\rhoab|=\numRhoABmedian$ and the Fisher matrix is
ill-conditioned ($\sim\!\numCondMedian$). Unlike the single-order wall, the degeneracy is
\emph{not} relieved by adding $b$-values; only a second diffusion time separates the orders
($|\rhoab|:\numBreakRhoSingle\!\to\!\numBreakRhoTwo$). Every bound is an
\emph{identifiability} statement scoped to its regime (forward model, SNR band, acquisition),
never an impossibility claim.
\end{abstract}

\section{Introduction}
\label{sec:intro}
Non-Gaussian diffusion in tissue is widely summarised by a fractional order. The
stretched-exponential model~\citep{bennett2003} writes the magnitude signal as
$S(b)=S_0\exp[-(bD)^\alpha]$, with $\alpha\in(0,1]$ a \emph{heterogeneity index}. The
continuous-time random-walk (CTRW) / fractional Bloch--Torrey
picture~\citep{magin2013} replaces the single exponent by two: a time-fractional (Caputo)
order $\alpha\in(0,1]$ encoding waiting-time heterogeneity and a space-fractional (Riesz)
order $\beta\in(1,2]$ encoding jump-length heterogeneity. These orders are reported as
biomarkers; the prior question---\emph{whether they are recoverable at all} under realistic
acquisition---is rarely priced.

We price it. The estimand is the fractional order, estimated \emph{jointly} with $D$ and $S_0$
from a finite set of $b$-values under Rician magnitude noise. The deliverable is a
\emph{recovery-collapse wall}: where, as a function of SNR and acquisition, the order ceases to
be usefully recoverable, located by the relative CRLB and corroborated by the parameter
correlations, the Fisher condition number, and profile-likelihood / parametric-bootstrap
confidence intervals.

\paragraph{What this is \emph{not} (positioning).}
The object is the Fisher information of the fractional order where the parameter enters the
likelihood \emph{only through the $b$-indexed signal attenuation} under Rician magnitude noise.
This is a different statistical experiment from three neighbours.
(i)~The Cram\'er--Rao bound for the self-similarity / Hurst exponent of fractional Brownian
motion~\citep{coeurjolly2001} is built from the \emph{trajectory / increment / mean-squared-%
displacement} likelihood of a self-similar \emph{process}: it has no $b$-value axis, no Rician
magnitude likelihood, and no joint $D$/$S_0$ nuisance structure. Same word
(``fractional exponent''), different likelihood.
(ii)~Empirical acquisition / quasi-diffusion-imaging design
studies~\citep{spilling2022} tabulate Monte-Carlo estimation error for chosen designs; we
instead \emph{derive} the information content in closed form and \emph{locate the wall}, with an
explicit $(n_b,b_{\max})$ surface and the $(\alpha,\beta)$ degeneracy structure.
(iii)~CRLB-based $b$-value optimisation for diffusion \emph{kurtosis}~\citep{poot2010} bounds a
\emph{cumulant} of a Taylor expansion, not a fractional order; the joint time/space-order
identifiability question has no kurtosis analogue.
This is not an estimator paper---we do not propose a better fitting method; we bound whether the
order is recoverable at all.

\section{Forward models and the information layer}
\label{sec:methods}

\subsection{Signal-decay forward models}
The single-order \emph{lead lane} is the stretched exponential~\citep{bennett2003}
\begin{equation}
S(b;S_0,D,\alpha)=S_0\,\exp\!\big[-(bD)^{\alpha}\big],\qquad \alpha\in(0,1].
\label{eq:stretched}
\end{equation}
For the joint model, the CTRW characteristic function~\citep{magin2013} is a Mittag-Leffler
function $\Ealpha$ of $|q|^\beta\,\Delta^{\alpha}$. At a single diffusion time $\Delta$, the
narrow-pulse relation $b\propto q^2\Delta$ gives $|q|^\beta\propto b^{\beta/2}$, so the signal
reduces to
\begin{equation}
S(b;S_0,D,\alpha,\beta)=S_0\,\Ealpha\!\big[-(bD)^{\beta/2}\big],
\label{eq:joint}
\end{equation}
with $\Ealpha(z)=\sum_{k\ge0}z^{k}/\Gamma(\alpha k+1)$ the one-parameter Mittag-Leffler
function. Equation~\eqref{eq:joint} reduces \emph{exactly} to the stretched
exponential~\eqref{eq:stretched} at $\alpha=1$ (since $E_1(z)=e^{z}$), with the heterogeneity
exponent equal to $\beta/2$: the single-order model is the time-order-one face of the joint
model. With a second diffusion time the attenuation carries the exponent $(\alpha-\beta/2)$ in
$\Delta$, which is what allows the two orders to be separated.

We evaluate $\Ealpha(-t)$, $t\ge0$, $0<\alpha<1$, by the Pollard/Mainardi completely-monotone
spectral integral~\citep{mainardi2010}, validated against the two closed forms
$E_1(-t)=e^{-t}$ and $E_{1/2}(-t)=e^{t^2}\mathrm{erfc}(t)$ and an independent adaptive
quadrature to $<5\times10^{-6}$; at $\alpha=1$ the exact exponential is used.

\subsection{Fisher information and the wall}
Magnitude data are Rician, $\sigma=S_0/\mathrm{SNR}$ at $b=0$. Because the parameters enter the
likelihood only through $\nu_i=S(b_i;\theta)$, the Fisher information matrix is
$\mathrm{FIM}(\theta)=\sum_i f(\nu_i/\sigma)\,\sigma^{-2}\,g_i g_i^{\top}$, where
$g_i=\partial S(b_i)/\partial\theta$ and $f(a)$ is the Rician information factor (which tends to
the Gaussian limit $1$ as $a\to\infty$). The CRLB is $\mathrm{cov}(\theta)\succeq
\mathrm{FIM}^{-1}$. We locate the single-order wall by the relative CRLB
$\cva=\sqrt{[\mathrm{FIM}^{-1}]_{\alpha\alpha}}/\alpha$ crossing a \emph{pre-registered}
threshold $\cva=\numCVthr$, corroborated by the $\alpha$--$D$ correlation and the FIM condition
number, and confirmed by a parametric bootstrap of the joint Rician maximum-likelihood
estimator. For the joint model the deliverable is the degeneracy structure of $(\alpha,\beta)$:
the correlation $\rhoab$ from $\mathrm{FIM}^{-1}$ ($\to\pm1$ signals inseparability) and the
condition number. The fractional order has no elementary Mittag-Leffler order-derivative, so the
joint Jacobian is taken by central finite differences (validated against the analytic
single-order Jacobian at the $\alpha=1,\beta=2$ reduction). All randomness is seeded; the
load-bearing intervals reproduce.

\section{Results}
\label{sec:results}

\subsection{The single-order wall (sparse clinical acquisition)}
At the canonical sparse clinical protocol ($n_b=\numCPzeroNb$, $b_{\max}=\numCPzeroBmax$\,s/mm$^2$,
$\alpha=\numCPzeroAlpha$, $D=\numCPzeroD\times10^{-3}$\,mm$^2$/s), the analytic CRLB wall is at
$\mathrm{SNR}^\ast=\numWallAnalytic$ and the empirical (bootstrap-MLE) wall at
$\mathrm{SNR}^\ast=\numWallEmp$, 95\,\% CI $[\numWallCIlo,\numWallCIhi]$---both \emph{inside} the
cited clinical-SNR band $[\numBandLo,\numBandHi]$ (Section~\ref{sec:band}). Extending $b_{\max}$
with few $b$-values trades against $\alpha$--$D$ collinearity ($\rho_{\alpha D}=\numRhoAD$ at
$n_b=4$, $b_{\max}=3000$, $\mathrm{SNR}=30$). The number of $b$-values is the dominant driver:
the wall recedes \emph{below} the clinical band only for dense research acquisition
($n_b\ge8$). Figure~\ref{fig:cp0}.

\subsection{Robustness across the physiological \texorpdfstring{$\alpha$}{alpha} range}
\label{sec:cp2}
Sweeping the wall across the physiological stretched-exponential range
$[\numAlphaPhysLo,\numAlphaPhysHi]$ at the sparsest clinical acquisition, the wall
$\mathrm{SNR}^\ast$ stays in $[\numWallAlphaLo,\numWallAlphaHi]$---inside the clinical band at
\emph{every} $\alpha$; $\alpha=\numCPzeroAlpha$ was not special, and the wall is slightly worse
toward low (more heterogeneous) $\alpha$. The pre-registered refute (the wall receding below the
band somewhere across $\alpha$) did not trigger: the ``clinically information-limited'' claim is
robust across $\alpha$, not an artefact of a single operating point.

\subsection{The joint time/space degeneracy (single diffusion time)}
\label{sec:cp1}
With both orders free at a single diffusion time, the joint Fisher matrix is severely degenerate
across the physiological grid: median $|\rhoab|=\numRhoABmedian$ (range
$[\numRhoABmin,\numRhoABmax]$) with condition number $\sim\!\numCondMedian$, exceeding the
pre-registered degeneracy threshold $\numDegenRho$. At the headline voxel
($\alpha=\numHeadAlpha$, $\beta=\numHeadBeta$) the analytic $\rhoab=\numHeadRhoAB$; the
parametric bootstrap ($\numNboot$ replicates) returns $\hat\alpha$ 95\,\% CI
$[\numBootAlphaLo,\numBootAlphaHi]$ and $\hat\beta$ 95\,\% CI $[\numBootBetaLo,\numBootBetaHi]$,
spanning nearly the entire admissible range---the practical face of the degeneracy.

Crucially, the degeneracy is \emph{not} relieved by adding $b$-values: $|\rhoab|$ moves only from
$\numNbRhoFour$ at $n_b=4$ to $\numNbRhoSixteen$ at $n_b=16$. This is the opposite of the
single-order wall (which recedes for $n_b\ge8$) and is the central distinction of the joint
problem: a single decay curve, however densely sampled in $b$, cannot disentangle a
Mittag-Leffler time-order from a $b$-power space-order. Only a \emph{second diffusion time}
breaks it: $|\rhoab|$ drops from $\numBreakRhoSingle$ to $\numBreakRhoTwo$ and the relative CRLB
$\cva$ from $\numBreakCVaSingle$ to $\numBreakCVaTwo$ (equal total measurement count).
Figure~\ref{fig:cp1}. This mechanistically explains why historical CTRW
characterisations~\citep{magin2013} separated the orders only by acquiring more than one
experiment family.

\subsection{The clinical-SNR band}
\label{sec:band}
The band $[\numBandLo,\numBandHi]$ is cited, not asserted: clinical diffusion-weighted imaging
reports $b=0$ SNR of order $40$ at 3\,T and $70$--$90$ at 7\,T~\citep{polders2011}, placing
routine $1.5$--$3$\,T acquisition in $[\numBandLo,\numBandHi]$ and research acquisition up to
$\sim\!100$. The walls of Sections~\ref{sec:results}--\ref{sec:cp2} sit inside this band; the
degeneracy of Section~\ref{sec:cp1} holds throughout it.

\section{Discussion}
\label{sec:discussion}
Two regime-scoped identifiability statements emerge. First, the single stretched-exponential
order is \emph{information-limited} under sparse clinical acquisition---its recovery wall lies
inside the clinical-SNR band across the physiological $\alpha$ range---and becomes recoverable
only with dense multi-$b$ research acquisition. Second, the joint CTRW time- and space-orders
are \emph{structurally degenerate} at a single diffusion time, and---unlike the single order---no
amount of $b$-sampling separates them; a second diffusion time is required. Both are
\emph{identifiability} statements scoped to the forward model, SNR band, and acquisition; neither
asserts that a parameter is unknowable in principle. The constructive boundaries ($n_b\ge8$ for
the single order; a second diffusion time for the joint orders) are stated, not hidden.

\paragraph{Limitations.} The forward models are single-compartment and single-diffusion-time
(except where a second time is explicitly added); the noise is Rician with known $\sigma$; the
analysis is simulation/theory, not an in-vivo study. The bootstrap operates inside box
constraints on $(\alpha,\beta)$, so the empirical $\hat\alpha,\hat\beta$ correlation
understates the analytic $\rhoab$---the marginal CIs spanning the admissible range are the
honest practical signal. The tooling for the reused Gr\"unwald--Letnikov operators and the
noise-amplification cross-check is forward-cited to a companion
release~\citep{ouroboros}. % FORWARD-CITE-ouroboros

\section{Conclusion}
Under the $b$-indexed Rician diffusion-MRI forward model, the stretched-exponential fractional
order is information-limited at clinically realistic acquisition, and the joint CTRW time/space
orders are degenerate at a single diffusion time in a way that more $b$-values cannot fix. These
are sharp, regime-scoped identifiability limits for fractional-order diffusion biomarkers, and
they point to the acquisition change (a second diffusion time) that would lift the joint
degeneracy.
