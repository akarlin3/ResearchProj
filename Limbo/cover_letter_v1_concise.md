Dear Editors,

I am pleased to submit "Trustworthy uncertainty quantification for quantitative and
diffusion body MRI, and its decision-use in MR-guided adaptive radiotherapy: a field
review" for consideration as a Review article in *Physics and Imaging in Radiation
Oncology*.

Adaptive MR-guided radiotherapy increasingly proposes to steer dose from a quantitative
or diffusion-MRI biomarker — a dose-painting prescription, or a response-adaptive change
to the plan. Acting on such a biomarker means acting on the uncertainty attached to it,
yet that uncertainty is seldom characterized rigorously enough to be trusted at the point
of a dose decision. This review surveys the path a biomarker error bar must travel before
it can change a dose: whether the bar is honest (calibrated and covering), whether a
trusted bar would change the decision, and what happens when it is deployed on an
MR-linac. It organizes the relevant work into a single taxonomy and maps four open
problems at the seams — conditional (not merely marginal) coverage; the step from
calibration to the dose decision; the propagation of biomarker uncertainty into dose; and
the estimator-dependence of trust, including its QIBA-metrology framing.

The synthesis is written for the radiation-oncology-physics readership that must make
these biomarker-to-dose judgments, which is why phiRO is its natural home. Rather than
advocate a method, it gives that readership a defensible map of what is and is not yet
established, and an agenda of the questions worth solving next. I make no claim to
completeness: this is a single-author narrative synthesis, not a systematic review, and I
state that limitation plainly in the manuscript.
